\section{Robustness}
\label{sec:robustness}

This section reports the robustness checks. The point-in-time (PIT) headquarters correction bounds the population scope of the result and is the binding check, so we report it in full; the remaining checks are summarized here, with detail in the Internet Appendix.

Two readings of the collapse are possible. A mechanism-strength reading says the channel operates most strongly on stable-HQ firms, where the local-investor unit of analysis is well-defined, so adding back the relocated firm-months dilutes the average. A sample-selection reading says the stable-HQ subsample is tilted toward firms with stronger anomaly moderation for reasons unrelated to literacy. The relocator characteristics in IA~\ref{ia:relocchar} (relocators are smaller, higher-IV, lower-IO, lower-return firms) favor the sample-selection reading. Either way, the characterization is a statement about the stable-HQ population, and generalizing to the corrected full panel is the main population-scope limitation.

\subsection{Time variation in the cross-state gradient}
\label{sec:robust_prepost}

The within-panel mid-IO three-way coefficient is essentially identical pre- and post-2015 ($\hat\gamma^{\text{pre}} = -0.024$, $\hat\gamma^{\text{post}} = -0.026$; difference $-0.002$, $t = +0.06$): the within-panel direction is stable. What varies across time is the \emph{cross-state} literacy gradient that interprets the result through the literacy mechanism. The pooled gradient is right-signed and sharp ($-0.0623$, state-$t = -5.01$), and the post-2015 sub-period is sharper still ($-0.0706$, $t = -3.50$); the pre-2015 sub-period is wrong-signed ($+0.0403$, $t = +1.89$) and concentrated in NFCS Wave 2009, the first administration of the Big-Five module (Waves 2012 to 2021 are directionally negative). The most likely explanation is measurement maturation in the later NFCS waves rather than a 2015-era regime change: institutional ownership of the panel rose post-2015 (mean $\Delta\,\mathtt{io\_share} = +0.083$), which rules out a retail-trading regime shift, and the state-level change in IO is uncorrelated with state literacy ($r = +0.04$). The sub-period table and a time-variation figure are in IA~\ref{ia:prepost} and IA~\ref{ia:gradfigs}.

\subsection{Additional checks}
\label{sec:robust_battery}

The remaining checks are reported in full in the Internet Appendix and summarized here.

\textit{Functional form.} The continuous-margin quadratic-IO clearance ($\hat\gamma_{5} = +0.0151$, $p = 0.017$) does not survive log-IO ($p = 0.75$), kink-IO ($p = 0.24$), or a flat twenty-bin diagnostic, so the discrete-tercile contrast of Table~\ref{tab:headline} carries the claim. The grid is IA~\ref{ia:formgrid}.

\textit{Instrumental variables.} The K-12 personal-finance mandate instrument is not usable: the strongest candidate has $r = -0.355$ (wrong sign) and $F = 7.06$, because low-literacy regions adopted mandates as a remedy, and a cohort-matched version with state, wave, and age-band fixed effects returns $F = 0.05$. OLS from Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl} is the preferred inferential statement, with first-stage and 2SLS detail in IA~\ref{ia:ivfirststage}.

\textit{Variance channel and education.} The mid-IO concentration is not a variance artifact, since mid-IO has lower identifying variation than low-IO yet a larger slope, and a state college-attainment horse-race within mid-IO leaves literacy sharp ($-0.0350$, $t = -2.97$) and college null ($+0.00076$, $t = +0.65$), so literacy is not a proxy for education. A within-retail literacy split inside the low-IO tercile adds nothing once decomposed across IV deciles (IA~\ref{ia:ivdecile}).

\textit{Inference, wave-anchor, and vintage.} One-way state clustering on 51 clusters via the wild-cluster bootstrap of \citet{cameronGelbachMiller2008} with Webb six-point weights \citep{webb2014} and the null imposed is the binding procedure throughout.\footnote{The cross-state gradient is interpreted alongside a state-permutation $p$-value ($B = 9{,}999$), with Holm step-down family-wise correction across the three jointly relevant tests. The two-way state-by-month CGM statistic is reported as a diagnostic in IA~\ref{ia:inference}.} Restricting to the five NFCS wave-anchor years sharpens the mid-IO coefficient ($-0.070$, $t = -2.09$), so the headline is not a forward-fill artifact, and fixing each firm's IO tercile to its 2009 assignment attenuates the mid/low ratio from $2.83$ to $2.09$ but preserves the concentration.
