\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{What the characterization changes}

The characterization positions state NFCS Big-Five literacy as a cross-sectional asset-pricing moderator that operates through an interaction-zone microfoundation, identified through the size and ownership margin rather than the IV margin the canonical limits-to-arbitrage reading predicts. Where household finance has studied literacy as a predictor of portfolio quality, participation, and resilience \citep{lusardiMitchell2014, calvetCampbellSodini2007, grinblattKeloharjuLinnainmaa2012}, this paper makes state-aggregate literacy a moderator on a specific anomaly interaction with a precisely characterized population scope. It adds a literacy moderator to the local-return-predictability program of \citet{korniotisKumar2013}: the two are distinct, since literacy is identified through IO and size conditioning the business cycle does not predict, but partly overlapping, since a business-cycle control block attenuates the literacy three-way by 20 to 23 percent.

Taking the size-controlled mid-IO coefficient $\hat\gamma_{\text{mid-IO}} = -0.0256$, the economic magnitude is a literacy-modulated annualized return differential of about $-5.5\%$ at a $0.187$-standard-deviation swing in the standardized composite. The non-size-controlled coefficient implies $-7\%$, and a classical errors-in-variables correction (reliability ratio $\lambda = 0.420$) extends the bracket to roughly $-13\%$. This $-5.5\%$ to $-13\%$ bracket overlaps the $\approx 5\%$ state-business-cycle strategy of \citet{korniotisKumar2013}.

\subsection{Limitations}

\paragraph{Population scope.} On the PIT-corrected full panel the mid-IO coefficient shrinks sharply, and the relocator characteristics (IA~\ref{ia:relocchar}) point toward a sample-selection rather than mechanism-strength reading of that collapse: relocators are smaller, higher-IV, lower-IO, lower-return firms, and about one-third of the stable-HQ mid/low gap comes from the low-IO comparator weakening under the relocator drop. The characterization is a statement about firms with stable headquarters across the panel window. Pre and post-relocation event studies on the 1{,}366 dropped firms would discriminate between the two readings.

\paragraph{Microfoundation and time variation.} The characterization is identified through the size-restriction migration and the cross-state gradient but does not separate the within-family readings (demand intensity, attention, timing), which the Boehmer-Jones-Zhang retail trade indicator could distinguish. The pooled cross-state gradient is driven mainly by post-2015 data; the pre-2015 slope is wrong-signed and concentrated in NFCS Wave 2009. Since institutional ownership rose post-2015 and is uncorrelated with the literacy gradient, and the within-panel mid-IO coefficient is essentially identical pre and post-2015, this looks like measurement noise in the first Big-Five administration rather than a substantive shift.

\paragraph{Identification.} The K-12 personal-finance mandate instrument is endogenous to state literacy through reverse causality, so the OLS estimate is the preferred inferential statement; a clean instrument would need pre-1990 mandate variation \citep{bernheimGarrettMaki2001} or verifiable-compliance cohort micro-data. The within-stock contrast also does not separate an ownership-interactive state-level confound, the leading instance being state-resident public-pension home-state ownership (about 21 managers in roughly seven states, collinear with the state fixed effect elsewhere), which would require a manager-level state-pension flag to close.
