\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The introduction asked where in the cross-section the literacy mechanism becomes visible. This section answers in four steps: the moderation concentrates in mid-IO firms and survives size as a competing moderator; the finding is a property not of mid-IO firms specifically but of an underlying economic population whose IO location shifts with the sample's size composition (the central mechanism evidence); that same population carries a cross-state literacy gradient in the predicted direction while firms outside it show none; and a parametric U-shape and a sample-selection decomposition close the section.

\subsection{Where the moderation lives: a U-shape in institutional ownership}
\label{sec:headline}

To see whether literacy moderates the momentum-IV interaction differently across firms, we discretize the within-stock IO distribution into terciles and estimate Equation~\eqref{eq:headline} within each on the stable-HQ subsample. The naive expectation is that the channel is strongest where retail dominates (low IO) and fades where institutions dominate (high IO), with the middle tercile ambiguous. Table~\ref{tab:headline} reports the result under persistent and time-varying IO measures.

The naive prediction is rejected. The low-IO tercile, the most retail-driven and supposedly most literacy-sensitive, returns a null coefficient, and the high-IO tercile is also null, consistent with institutional dominance neutralizing the local-state channel. The moderation concentrates in the middle: mid-IO is the only tercile with state-clustered $|t| > 2$ on both IO measures, with magnitude roughly three times the low-IO and six times the high-IO estimates. This discrete contrast does not depend on a functional form for IO and is the central characterization; Section~\ref{sec:quadratic} reports a continuous quadratic specification that corroborates the U-shape but does not replace it.

\subsection{Is the U-shape just a size effect?}
\label{sec:size_controlled_result}

Mid-IO firms are also concentrated in the middle of the market-capitalization distribution (median ME \$561 million, versus \$66 million for low-IO and \$1.40 billion for high-IO), so a reader could wonder whether the finding is a size effect captured by the IO discretization. The size-controlled specification (Equation~\ref{eq:size_ctrl}) tests this by entering size as a parallel moderator on the same anomaly, with the same standardization and interaction structure as literacy: if the mid-IO three-way is mechanically conflated with size, the literacy coefficient should attenuate to insignificance; if literacy is distinct, it should survive. Table~\ref{tab:size_ctrl} reports the comparison.

Literacy survives. The mid-IO three-way attenuates about twenty percent (to $-0.0256$) but stays significant at $t = -2.24$, while size's own three-way within mid-IO is statistically zero: conditional on being inside the mid-IO tercile, the literacy moderation is identified independently of size. The specification also sharpens the U-shape, turning the already-small high-IO coefficient into a clean null ($-0.0001$, $t = -0.00$).

\subsection{The migration test: what moves when the sample changes}
\label{sec:migration}

The size-controlled result tells us the moderation lives in mid-IO but not \emph{why}. A natural reading is that mid-IO is simply where the full panel's size composition puts the interaction zone, so the moderation should follow that zone as the sample changes rather than being a fixed property of the tercile. Restricting to larger firms removes the micro-caps that populate low-IO and shifts the conditional IO distribution upward; under the interaction-zone reading, firms that occupied mid-IO at the full-panel margin should reappear at low-IO in the restricted sample, carrying the moderation with them.

Table~\ref{tab:migration} tests this: Panel A reports the within-panel three-way coefficient under progressive size filters, and Panel B the cross-state literacy gradient in the relevant cell under each filter.

The prediction holds cell by cell. In the full panel the moderation lives in mid-IO; dropping the bottom twenty percent of firms by market cap \emph{strengthens} the mid-IO coefficient (the micro-caps removed fall predominantly in low-IO); and dropping the bottom fifty percent relocates the significant coefficient entirely to low-IO ($\hat\gamma_{\text{IO1}} = -0.0441$, $t = -2.04$), with mid-IO now null. The within-panel finding is not a property of mid-IO firms but of an underlying population whose IO label depends on what else is in the sample. The cross-state gradient migrates with it: it lives in mid-IO in the full panel ($r = -0.28$, $t = -5.01$) and in low-IO in the above-median sample ($r = -0.29$, $t = -4.30$), while high-IO shows none under either composition, the null the reading predicts. The migration is the signature an interaction-zone microfoundation predicts and the IO-tercile result alone does not deliver.

\subsection{Across states, does the moderation track literacy?}
\label{sec:cross_state_grad}

A direct cross-sectional test asks whether, across firms in the interaction-zone cell, the moderation tracks state literacy in the predicted direction. We estimate the three-way coefficient state by state under Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl} restricted to that cell, then regress the per-state coefficients on state NFCS literacy with precision weighting:

\begin{equation}
\hat\gamma_{\text{lit}, s} = \alpha + \beta \cdot \mathtt{literacy}_{s} + u_{s}, \qquad \hat\beta = -0.0623, \; \mathrm{SE} = 0.0124, \; t = -5.01, \; \text{Pearson } r = -0.275.
\end{equation}

The gradient runs in the predicted direction: higher-literacy states show more negative per-state coefficients, with a state-permutation two-sided $p = 0.0047$. Across the three jointly relevant tests, the headline coefficient, the high-IO coefficient, and the cross-state slope, Holm step-down family-wise $p$-values are $0.05$, $0.014$, and $0.996$ respectively: the headline and the gradient survive multiplicity correction, and the high-IO cell is empty.

The same negative slope appears in two settings, the full-panel mid-IO cell and the above-median IO1 cell, recovering essentially the same gradient across two IO terciles in two sample restrictions (IA~\ref{ia:gradfigs}), as the migration story predicts. Figure~\ref{fig:migration} consolidates the migration evidence: the within-panel coefficients across the four size filters, and the cross-state gradient scatters for the original headline cell, the cell that loses the gradient when smaller firms are removed, and the migrated headline cell. The interaction zone moves with the sample, and the gradient moves with it.

\subsection{A continuous corroboration of the U-shape}
\label{sec:quadratic}

The discrete-tercile contrast is the headline result. A natural follow-up asks whether a continuous functional form on IO recovers the same non-monotone shape with a separable interior trough. Augmenting Equation~\eqref{eq:headline} with linear and quadratic IO interactions produces the test:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:quadIO}
r_{i,s,t} = \alpha_{s} + \tau_{t}
+ \gamma_{4}\,(\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}\cdot\mathrm{IO})
+ \gamma_{5}\,(\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}\cdot\mathrm{IO}^{2})
+ \boldsymbol{\beta}'\mathbf{X}_{i,s,t} + \varepsilon_{i,s,t},
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{X}$ collects the 21 lower-order interactions and the literacy slope $s(\mathrm{IO}) = \gamma_{3} + \gamma_{4}\,\mathrm{IO} + \gamma_{5}\,\mathrm{IO}^{2}$ has an interior minimum at $\mathrm{IO}^{\ast} = -\gamma_{4}/(2\gamma_{5})$ when $\gamma_{5} > 0$. On the stable-HQ persistent measure $\hat\gamma_{5} = +0.0151$ ($t = +2.28$, wcb $p = 0.017$), with implied trough $\mathrm{IO}^{\ast} = 0.344$, inside the mid-IO tercile and where the discrete contrast predicts. The clearance rests on a single functional form, since log-IO and kink-IO specifications return insignificant curvature (Section~\ref{sec:robustness}), so the discrete-tercile contrast remains the robust claim and the quadratic is corroboration rather than a separate inferential statement.

\subsection{How much of the contrast comes from sample selection?}
\label{sec:relocator}

The headline drops relocators, so part of the mid-vs-low contrast reflects the firms excluded. The mid/low coefficient ratio is $2.83$ on the stable-HQ panel against $1.09$ on the full panel including relocators. A firm-by-firm decomposition (IA~\ref{ia:relocchar}) attributes about one-third of the gap to the low-IO comparator weakening under the relocator drop (relocators are nearly twice as concentrated in low-IO, with a near-zero relocator-only low-IO coefficient) and about two-thirds to mid-IO strengthening. Relocators are smaller, higher-IV, lower-IO, lower-return firms, so part of the headline magnitude reflects sample composition. Section~\ref{sec:robustness} takes up the population-scope question directly through the point-in-time literacy reassignment on the full panel.
