\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A large literature documents that less financially literate investors trade systematically worse, and a separate literature documents that retail demand is geographically concentrated and moves prices. Together they predict that state-level investor literacy should moderate the return anomalies that depend on retail mispricing. Aggregate tests deliver weak signals; the contribution of this paper is to show that the literacy mechanism is not absent but hidden by aggregation, and to characterize precisely where in the cross-section --- and under what conditions on ownership and size --- it operates.

The data identify an interaction zone: the literacy channel leaves a price footprint only where retail demand is large enough to move prices and institutional activity is sufficient to translate it into return differences detectable across states. Where neither holds (micro-caps held mostly by retail) mispricing accumulates without loading on state literacy; where institutions dominate (large high-IO firms) arbitrage neutralizes the local-state channel. In the full stable-HQ panel the zone falls in mid-IO; restricting to larger firms relocates it to low-IO, and the cross-state literacy gradient migrates with it, while firms outside the zone show no gradient. This migration --- the moderation moving with sample composition --- is the central new piece of evidence; it identifies the interaction-zone reading without an instrument, which is fortunate because the natural candidate (state K-12 personal-finance mandates) fails its first-stage relevance test for documented reasons. The result rests on OLS under conditional unconfoundedness, with the migration providing the predicted-signature confirmation an instrument would otherwise supply.

Three honest bounds discipline the result: the U-shape clears five percent on a quadratic form only (the discrete-tercile contrast is the functional-form-free claim); the point-in-time literacy reassignment collapses the headline magnitude roughly twenty-six fold, leaving the stable-headquarters subsample as the population for which the characterization holds; and the within-mid-IO IV-decile decomposition rejects the textbook limits-to-arbitrage reading, so the supported microfoundation is identified through size and ownership conditioning, not the IV margin. Whether other state-level investor traits --- participation rates, brokerage density, retail-attention measures --- operate through a similar interaction zone is a natural next question, and the size-restriction migration test developed here provides a portable signature for testing it.
