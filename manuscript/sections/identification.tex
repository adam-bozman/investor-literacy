\section{Identification}
\label{sec:identification}

The introduction posed two questions: where in the cross-section the literacy mechanism becomes visible, and what microfoundation that location identifies. This section describes the design that answers them.

\subsection{What the research question requires}

Testing whether state literacy moderates the momentum-IV anomaly, more strongly in literate states, means measuring how the within-stock relationship between returns and $\mathrm{mom}\times\mathrm{IV}$ varies with the headquarters-state literacy of each stock. Three features of the data dictate the design. First, literacy is measured at the state-wave level (the FINRA NFCS fields the Big-Five questions every three years, five wave values over 2009 to 2023), while anomaly returns are at the firm-month level; bridging the scales requires the identifying variation to be cross-firm \emph{within} state-month cells, not cross-state (which would conflate literacy with other state-level return determinants) and not cross-time (which would conflate it with aggregate dynamics). Second, the channel transmits through local-investor community ties that take time to form and decay, so for firms that relocate headquarters the effective local base is in transition; a clean design restricts to stable-headquarters firms and reports the static versus point-in-time correction as a falsification check. Third, the prediction concerns how the moderation varies with ownership structure, so the design stratifies by within-stock institutional-ownership tercile rather than collapsing across IO levels.

\subsection{The baseline specification}

The headline specification implements these requirements as a two-way fixed-effects panel regression:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:headline}
\begin{split}
r_{i,s,t} = {}& \alpha_{s} + \tau_{t} + \gamma \,\bigl(\mathrm{mom}_{i,t}\cdot\mathrm{IV}_{i,t}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}_{s(i,t),t}\bigr) \\
& + \delta_{1}\,(\mathrm{mom}_{i,t}\cdot\mathrm{IV}_{i,t}) + \delta_{2}\,(\mathrm{mom}_{i,t}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}_{s(i,t),t}) + \delta_{3}\,(\mathrm{IV}_{i,t}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}_{s(i,t),t}) \\
& + \boldsymbol{\beta}'\mathbf{X}_{i,s,t} + \varepsilon_{i,s,t}.
\end{split}
\end{equation}

The dependent variable $r_{i,s,t}$ is the month-$t$ return of firm $i$ headquartered in state $s(i,t)$. State fixed effects $\alpha_{s}$ absorb time-invariant state characteristics; month fixed effects $\tau_{t}$ absorb aggregate shocks. The three two-way interactions enter as controls; the three-way coefficient $\gamma$ is the object of interest. The state-assignment function $s(i,t)$ is the static Compustat snapshot under the headline merge and the point-in-time EDGAR 10-K-header reassignment under the falsification check; on the stable-headquarters subsample the two coincide. The discrete-tercile rendering of Equation~\eqref{eq:headline} re-estimates $\gamma$ within each within-stock IO tercile.

\subsection{Adding size as a moderator: why and how}

Mid-IO firms are concentrated in mid-cap market-equity quintiles (median market capitalization roughly five to ten times higher than in low-IO), so a reader could ask whether the mid-IO three-way is a size moderation captured by the IO discretization. The size-controlled specification rules this out by entering size as a parallel moderator on the same anomaly:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:size_ctrl}
\begin{split}
r_{i,s,t} = {}& \alpha_{s} + \tau_{t} + \gamma \,\bigl(\mathrm{mom}_{i,t}\cdot\mathrm{IV}_{i,t}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}_{s(i,t),t}\bigr) \\
& + \zeta \,\bigl(\mathrm{mom}_{i,t}\cdot\mathrm{IV}_{i,t}\cdot\mathtt{size\_z}_{i,t}\bigr) \\
& + \text{lower-order literacy interactions} + \text{lower-order size interactions} + \varepsilon_{i,s,t},
\end{split}
\end{equation}

where $\mathtt{size\_z}_{i,t}$ is the within-month $z$-score of $\log(\mathrm{ME}_{i,t})$, entered with the same standardization and interaction structure as literacy. The coefficient $\gamma$ in Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl} is identified holding size moderation of the same anomaly fixed: if the literacy moderation is mechanically conflated with size through the mid-IO sample's mid-cap concentration, $\gamma$ should attenuate to insignificance; if literacy is a distinct moderator, $\gamma$ should survive. Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl} is the preferred specification throughout.

\subsection{Identifying assumptions}

For $\gamma$ to be interpretable as a literacy moderation, three assumptions must hold.

\paragraph{Conditional unconfoundedness for the literacy moderator.} Conditional on state and month fixed effects, the three two-way interactions, and the size moderator in Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl}, residual variation in state-aggregate literacy is uncorrelated with omitted return determinants that interact with $\mathrm{mom}\times\mathrm{IV}$ in the same functional form. The paper addresses this four ways: a Fama-French 12-industry control block; a state college-attainment horse-race within mid-IO (literacy three-way $-0.0350$, $t = -2.97$; college $+0.00076$, $t = +0.65$); the within-stock IO contrast, which discriminates against any generic state-level moderator hitting all headquarters-state stocks alike; and the size-controlled specification, which discriminates against the most natural firm-level moderator. What remains is a class of ownership-interactive state-level confounds the design does not separate, which Section~\ref{sec:discussion} takes up.

\paragraph{Correct headquarters-state assignment.} The static Compustat snapshot mis-assigns relocator firm-months for the 14 percent of observations whose static state differs from the then-current 10-K headquarters state. Headline patterns are estimated on the stable-HQ subsample, for which the static and point-in-time merges coincide; the PIT reassignment on the full panel is the strictest available correction, and the resulting magnitude collapse (Section~\ref{sec:robustness}) is the binding population-scope limitation.

\paragraph{Cluster-robust inference with 51 state clusters.} The natural clustering dimension is the state, and with 51 clusters asymptotic cluster-robust standard errors over-reject; the binding procedure is the one-way state-level wild-cluster bootstrap of \citet{cameronGelbachMiller2008} with Webb six-point weights \citep{webb2014} and the null imposed.

\subsection{Causal language}

Under conditional unconfoundedness, $\hat\gamma$ in Equation~\eqref{eq:size_ctrl} recovers the average within-state-month differential sensitivity of returns to $\mathrm{mom}\times\mathrm{IV}$ as a function of state-aggregate literacy; it is the conditional association the design supports, not a clean average treatment effect.\footnote{A natural instrument for state literacy is state K-12 personal-finance graduation requirements (building on Urban-Schmeiser 2015/2020), but it is not usable here: mandate states are systematically less literate, since states adopted mandates in response to low adult literacy, so the cross-state first stage is wrong-signed, and a cohort-matched version with state, wave, and age-band fixed effects yields $F = 0.05$. The OLS estimate is the preferred inferential statement. Detail is in IA~\ref{ia:ivfirststage} and Section~\ref{sec:robustness}.}
