State Financial Literacy and the\\
Cross-Section of the Momentum Anomaly}
\author{Adam Bozman\thanks{Washington State University. Email: \texttt{bozmanadam@gmail.com}.}}
\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Financial literacy varies widely across U.S. states, and less literate investors are known to make worse portfolio choices. If those choices move prices, state literacy should moderate the return anomalies driven by retail mispricing. In aggregate the signal is weak, but I show it is not absent so much as hidden by aggregation. On the stable-headquarters subsample of CRSP common stock from 2009 to 2023, the literacy moderation of the momentum and idiosyncratic-volatility interaction concentrates in firms with mid-level institutional ownership: the segment with enough retail demand to move prices and enough institutional activity to register that demand in returns across states. Firms held mostly by retail and firms dominated by institutions show no moderation. Restricting the sample to larger firms moves the moderation to the low-ownership tercile, and the cross-state literacy gradient moves with it. This migration with size composition is the paper's main piece of evidence: it locates where the mechanism operates without relying on an instrument, and it bounds the result to firms whose headquarters, and hence local investor base, stay put.
\end{abstract}
