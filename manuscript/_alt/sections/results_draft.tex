\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

This section establishes the headline empirical fact and disciplines it. Subsection~\ref{sec:headline} reports the discrete-tercile mid-IO concentration on the stable-HQ subsample, which is the load-bearing functional-form-free characterization. Subsection~\ref{sec:quadratic} reports the continuous-margin quadratic specification that corroborates the U-shape. Subsections~\ref{sec:relocator} and~\ref{sec:prepost} bound the result: the relocator decomposition shows how much of the stable-HQ mid/low ratio comes from sample selection, and the pre/post-2015 split tests temporal stability. Subsection~\ref{sec:withinretail} reports a within-retail null that is itself an aggregation artifact. Subsection~\ref{sec:nestedbaseline} reports the point-in-time literacy reassignment under which the seed's specific aggregate prediction collapses. Subsection~\ref{sec:variance} reports the variance and college-attainment diagnostics that rule out two purely statistical readings of the mid-IO concentration.

The mid-IO tercile is the only one with state-clustered $|t|>2$ on both IO measures and the only one clearing the ten-percent wild-cluster bootstrap threshold ($p = 0.064$ persistent, $p = 0.058$ time-varying). The low- and high-IO coefficients are roughly one-third and one-sixth of the mid magnitude. This discrete-tercile contrast is functional-form-free: it discretizes the within-stock IO distribution into three buckets and reports the coefficient on each. It is the load-bearing characterization of the headline fact, and the continuous-margin quadratic in Subsection~\ref{sec:quadratic} corroborates rather than replaces it.

\subsection{Quadratic-IO corroboration of the U-shape}
\label{sec:quadratic}

Augmenting the headline three-way with a quadratic-IO interaction on the stable-HQ subsample,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:quadIO}
r_{i,s,t} = \alpha_{s} + \tau_{t}
+ \gamma_{4}\,(\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}\cdot\mathrm{IO})
+ \gamma_{5}\,(\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}\cdot\mathrm{IO}^{2})
+ \boldsymbol{\beta}'\mathbf{X}_{i,s,t} + \varepsilon_{i,s,t},
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{X}$ collects the 21 lower-order interactions, gives a literacy slope on $\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}$ of $s(\mathrm{IO}) = \gamma_{3} + \gamma_{4}\,\mathrm{IO} + \gamma_{5}\,\mathrm{IO}^{2}$, with an interior minimum at $\mathrm{IO}^{\ast} = -\gamma_{4}/(2\gamma_{5})$ when $\gamma_{5} > 0$. Table~\ref{tab:headline_quadratic} reports the result.

On the persistent measure, $\hat\gamma_{5} = +0.0151$ clears wild-cluster bootstrap $p = 0.017$, and the implied trough $\mathrm{IO}^{\ast} = 0.344$ sits inside the mid-IO mean share of $0.495$. The U-shape characterization is therefore consistent across the discrete-tercile and quadratic specifications. The inferential statement that does not depend on the parametric form is the discrete-tercile $p = 0.064$ (individual) and $p = 0.077$ (family-wise).

Relocators are nearly twice as concentrated in the low-IO tercile ($24.6$ percent) as in the other two ($\approx 13.7$--$13.8$ percent), and the relocator-only low-IO coefficient is approximately zero. Dropping relocators therefore both attenuates the low-IO coefficient (from $-0.0170$ to $-0.0113$, a $34$-percent reduction) and grows the mid-IO coefficient (from $-0.0185$ to $-0.0321$, a $73$-percent increase). About one-third of the stable-HQ mid/low gap comes from low-IO attenuation; about two-thirds comes from mid-IO growth. Relocators are smaller, higher-IV, and lower-return firms (IA~\ref{ia:relocchar}), a pattern consistent with a sample-selection contribution to the headline; the magnitude reading is engaged in Subsection~\ref{sec:nestedbaseline}.

\subsection{Within-retail wrong sign}
\label{sec:withinretail}

Within the stable-HQ low-IO tercile, splitting at the within-tercile state-mean literacy median delivers a low-literacy-minus-high-literacy difference in the three-way of $+0.0033$ persistent (state-clustered $t = +2.80$, wild-cluster bootstrap $p = 0.024$) and $+0.0029$ time-varying ($t = +2.00$, $p = 0.093$). The sign runs opposite to the naive sophistication reading, in which the literacy gradient should be most negative where retail dominates.

Decomposing the difference across within-month NYSE IV deciles (IA~\ref{ia:ivdecile}) shows the result is an aggregation artifact: five deciles return positive differences and five return negative, only decile 8 is individually significant, and the observation-weighted average is $+0.0002$. The aggregate $+0.0033$ is attributable to observation-weighting alone and decomposes to approximately zero across IV deciles. The sign reversal therefore provides no positive evidence against the naive sophistication reading; nor does it discriminate among within-family candidates. A similar IV-decile decomposition within mid-IO is reported in Subsection~\ref{sec:variance} alongside the mechanism diagnostics.

Under the seed's static-snapshot merge, the full-panel aggregate three-way is $-0.0117$ at marginal significance ($p = 0.078$). Under the PIT reassignment, it collapses to $-0.0003$ ($p = 0.517$) --- approximately thirty-nine times smaller. The within-stock retail-versus-institutional difference moves from $-0.0211$ to $+0.00029$, a sign flip. The seed's specific aggregate prediction is an artifact of pairing relocator firm-months with the literacy of their static-snapshot headquarters state rather than their then-current state.

Panel B records the corresponding collapse in the mid-IO coefficient on the PIT full panel: from $-0.0321$ on stable-HQ to $-0.00121$ on PIT full-panel, approximately twenty-six times smaller. The within-stock tercile pattern is directionally preserved --- mid-IO remains the only tercile with state-clustered $|t| \geq 2$ on both IO measures --- but the magnitudes are not. The PIT magnitude collapse is the binding population-scope limitation: the headline holds on the stable-HQ subsample, where the local-investor unit of analysis is held fixed, and is roughly twenty-six times smaller on the corrected full panel. Two readings of the gap are admissible under the design: that the mechanism operates most strongly on stable-HQ firms, or that the stable-HQ subsample is sample-selected toward where the result is strongest. The relocator characteristics in IA~\ref{ia:relocchar} lean toward the sample-selection reading; the mechanism-strength reading of the magnitude collapse is not defendable on current evidence.

\subsection{Variance-channel diagnostic and college horse-race}
\label{sec:variance}
\label{sec:college}

A purely statistical reading would attribute the mid-IO concentration to greater identifying variation at the mid tercile. The data rule this out for the comparison that matters. Within-tercile variances of the focal triple $\mathrm{mom}\cdot\mathrm{IV}\cdot\mathtt{literacy\_z}$ on the stable-HQ subsample are $1.664$ (low-IO), $0.673$ (mid-IO), and $0.434$ (high-IO); Bartlett's equal-variance test rejects strongly, and the same ordering survives state-month demeaning. Mid-IO has lower identifying variation than low-IO yet a larger slope, so the variance channel cannot explain the mid-vs-low contrast. It is consistent with the mid-vs-high ranking, but that ranking is not the discriminating margin.

State financial literacy correlates with state college attainment at $\rho = 0.62$, so a second purely statistical reading is that the literacy variable is a stand-in for education. A horse-race within mid-IO returns $\hat\gamma_{\text{lit}} = -0.0350$ ($t = -2.97$) and $\hat\gamma_{\text{college}} = +0.00076$ ($t = +0.65$): the literacy three-way sharpens relative to the literacy-only mid-IO baseline of $-0.0321$, while the college three-way is opposite-signed and indistinguishable from zero. On the stable-HQ aggregate, $\hat\gamma_{\text{lit}} = -0.0122$ ($t = -1.65$) and $\hat\gamma_{\text{college}} = -0.00051$ ($t = -0.63$). Literacy is not a proxy for college attainment within mid-IO.
