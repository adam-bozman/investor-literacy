\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A large literature documents that less financially literate investors trade systematically worse, and a separate literature documents that retail demand is geographically concentrated and moves prices. Together they predict that state-level investor literacy should moderate the return anomalies that depend on retail mispricing. Aggregate tests deliver weak signals; the contribution of this paper is to show that the literacy mechanism is not absent but hidden by aggregation, and to characterize where in the cross-section, and under what conditions on ownership and size, it operates.

The data identify an interaction zone. The literacy channel leaves a price footprint only where retail demand is large enough to move prices and institutional activity is sufficient to translate it into return differences across states. Where neither holds (micro-caps held mostly by retail) mispricing accumulates without loading on state literacy; where institutions dominate (large high-IO firms) arbitrage neutralizes the local-state channel. In the full stable-HQ panel the zone falls in mid-IO; restricting to larger firms moves it to low-IO, and the cross-state literacy gradient moves with it, while firms outside the zone show no gradient. This migration with sample composition is the paper's central piece of evidence, and it identifies the interaction-zone reading without relying on an instrument. The result rests on OLS under conditional unconfoundedness, with the migration supplying the predicted-signature confirmation an instrument would otherwise provide.

The characterization holds for firms with stable headquarters; under the point-in-time literacy reassignment the headline magnitude shrinks, which bounds the population to which it applies, and the U-shape across the IO distribution is robust as a discrete-tercile contrast rather than as a parametric form. Whether other state-level investor traits such as participation rates, brokerage density, or retail-attention measures operate through a similar interaction zone is a natural next question, and the size-restriction migration test developed here gives a portable way to look.
